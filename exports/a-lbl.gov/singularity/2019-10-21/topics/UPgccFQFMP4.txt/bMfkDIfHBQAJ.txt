Call for Papers

Virtualization technologies constitute a key enabling factor for flexible=
=20
resource management

in modern data centers, and particularly in cloud environments. Cloud=20
providers need to

manage complex infrastructures in a seamless fashion to support the highly=
=20
dynamic and

heterogeneous workloads and hosted applications customers deploy.=20
Similarly, HPC

environments have been increasingly adopting techniques that enable=20
flexible management

of vast computing and networking resources, close to marginal provisioning=
=20
cost, which is

unprecedented in the history of scientific and commercial computing.

Various virtualization technologies contribute to the overall picture in=20
different ways: machine

virtualization, with its capability to enable consolidation of multiple=20
under=C2=ADutilized servers with

heterogeneous software and operating systems (OSes), and its capability to=
=20
live=C2=AD-migrate a

fully operating virtual machine (VM) with a very short downtime, enables=20
novel and dynamic

ways to manage physical servers; OS-=C2=ADlevel virtualization (i.e.,=20
containerization), with its

capability to isolate multiple user=C2=AD-space environments and to allow f=
or=20
their co=C2=ADexistence

within the same OS kernel, promises to provide many of the advantages of=20
machine=20

virtualization with high levels of responsiveness and performance; I/O=20
Virtualization allows=20

physical network interfaces to take traffic from multiple VMs or=20
containers; network=20

virtualization, with its capability to create logical network overlays that=
=20
are independent of the

underlying physical topology is furthermore enabling virtualization of HPC=
=20
infrastructures.=20

Publication

Accepted papers will be published in a Springer LNCS proceedings volume.


Topics of Interest

The VHPC program committee solicits original, high-quality submissions=20
related to

virtualization across the entire software stack with a special focus on the=
=20
intersection of HPC

and the cloud.

Major Topics

- Virtualization in supercomputing environments, HPC clusters, HPC in the=
=20
cloud and grids
- OS-level virtualization and containers (LXC, Docker, rkt, Singularity,=20
Shifter, i.a.)
- Lightweight/specialized operating systems in conjunction with virtual=20
machines

- Novel unikernels and use cases for virtualized HPC environments

- Performance improvements for or driven by unikernels

- Tool support for unikernels: configuration/build environments, debuggers,=
=20
profilers

- Hypervisor extensions to mitigate side-channel attacks=20

 ([micro-]architectural timing attacks, privilege escalation)

- VM & Container trust and security

- Containers inside VMs with hypervisor isolation

- GPU virtualization operationalization

- Approaches to GPGPU virtualization including API remoting and hypervisor=
=20
abstraction
- Optimizations of virtual machine monitor platforms and hypervisors
- Hypervisor support for heterogeneous resources (GPUs, co-processors,=20
FPGAs, etc.)
- Virtualization support for emerging memory technologies

- Virtualization in enterprise HPC and microvisors
- Software defined networks and network virtualization
- Management, deployment of virtualized environments and orchestration=20
(Kubernetes i.a.)
- Workflow-pipeline container-based composability=20

- Checkpointing facilitation utilizing containers and VMs=20

- Emerging topics including multi-kernel approaches and NUMA in hypervisors

- Operating MPI in containers/VMs and Unikernels =20

- Virtualization in data intensive computing (big data) - HPC convergence
- Adaptation of HPC technologies in the cloud (high performance networks,=
=20
RDMA, etc.)
- Performance measurement, modelling and monitoring of virtualized/cloud=20
workloads

- Latency-and jitter sensitive workloads in virtualized/containerized=20
environments
- I/O virtualization (including applications, SR-IOV, i.a.)=20

- Hybrid local facility + cloud compute and based storage systems,=20
cloudbursting

- FPGA and many-core accelerator virtualization
- Job scheduling/control/policy and container placement in virtualized=20
environments
- Cloud reliability, fault-tolerance and high-availability
- QoS and SLA in virtualized environments
- IaaS platforms, cloud frameworks and APIs
- Energy-efficient and power-aware virtualization

- Configuration management tools for containers (including in OpenStack,=20
Ansible, i.a.)

- ARM-based hypervisors, ARM virtualization extensions



Special Track: GPU - Accelerator Virtualization

GPU virtualization technologies, performance and benchmarking, integration=
=20
with

workflow scheduling systems, integration to cluster managers.

GPUs are taking on many HPC workload areas, especially in deep learning=20
within

machine learning. In addition, a lot of workload is being pushed to elastic=
=20
environments=20

utilizing various virtualization technologies on different levels like=20
hypervisors=20

(e.g. VMWare, Xen, KVM), kernel (Docker, Kubernetes) or on the resource=20
manager

level (YARN, Mesos). In this track we invite submissions addressing these=
=20
problems.=20

Paper Submission-Publication

Papers submitted to the workshop will be reviewed by at least two

members of the program committee and external reviewers. Submissions

should include abstract, keywords, the e-mail address of the

corresponding author, and must not exceed 10 pages, including tables

and figures at a main font size no smaller than 11 point. Submission

of a paper should be regarded as a commitment that, should the paper

be accepted, at least one of the authors will register and attend the

conference to present the work. Accepted papers will be published in a

Springer LNCS volume. .=20

The format must be according to the Springer LNCS Style. Initial

submissions are in PDF; authors of accepted papers will be requested

to provide source files.
